4/4/2013   CR-10-0362STATE OF ARIZONA v EDWARD JAMES ROSEOPINION
 John Pelander, Author; Rebecca White Berch, Concur, Scott Bales, Concur, Robert M. Brutinel, Concur, Ann A. Scott Timmer, Concur

Constitutionality Decision
Holding A.R.S. § 21-202(B)(3) (requiring dismissal of prospective jurors “not currently capable of understanding the English language”) constitutional and not violative of U.S. Const. amend. 6 (right to jury trial) or U.S. Const. amend. 14 (due process, equal protection).  Holding A.R.S. § 13-752(R) (authorizing victim impact statements in capital cases) constitutional and not violative of U.S. Const. amend. 8 (cruel and unusual punishment) or U.S. Const. amend. 14 (due process).   Holding A.R.S. § 13-751(F)(10) (providing as an aggravating circumstance in a capital case that the victim “was an on duty peace officer who was killed in the course of performing the officer’s official duties and the defendant knew, or should have known, that the murdered person was a peace officer”) constitutional and not violative of U.S. Const. amend. 8 (cruel and unusual punishment) or U.S. Const. amend 14 (due process, equal protection).    Holding A.R.S. § 13-756(A) (providing for abuse of discretion appellate review in capital cases) constitutional and not violative of U.S. Const. amend. 8 (cruel and unusual punishment) or U.S. Const. amend. 14 (due process).
